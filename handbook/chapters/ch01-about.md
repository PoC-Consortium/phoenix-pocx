# About this Handbook

Welcome to the *Phoenix PoCX Wallet User Handbook*. This guide walks you through everything you need to use Phoenix PoCX with confidence — from installing the wallet for the first time to running a fully configured mining rig.

## Who this handbook is for

The handbook is written for two kinds of reader.

If you want to **send, receive, and manage Bitcoin-PoCX**, you are in the right place. Parts I and II take you from installation through everyday use, with a short primer on what Bitcoin-PoCX is and why it works the way it does.

If you also want to **mine** — that is, commit storage space to the network and earn rewards in return — Part III is dedicated to you. It explains the underlying ideas, walks you through generating plot files on both CPU and GPU, and shows how to monitor your miner from inside the wallet.

You do not need a background in cryptocurrencies to follow along. Whenever a concept is specific to Bitcoin-PoCX — *plotting*, *forging*, *deadlines*, and so on — it is explained the first time it appears, and gathered in the glossary at the back of the handbook for quick reference.

## How this handbook is organised

The handbook is divided into five parts:

| Part | Title             | What it covers                                                            |
|:----:|-------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I    | Getting Started   | Installation, first launch, creating your wallet                          |
| II   | Using Your Wallet | Sending, receiving, transaction history, settings, security               |
| III  | Mining & Plotting | Everything from your first plot file to a multi-chain mining setup        |
| IV   | Special Topics    | Mining on Android, the multi-rig aggregator, running your own node        |
| V    | Help & Reference  | Troubleshooting, frequently asked questions, glossary, where to get help  |

If you only intend to use the wallet, you can stop after Part II. If you want to mine, complete Parts I and II first to get the wallet working, then continue with Part III.

You do not need to read the handbook front to back. Each chapter is self-contained, and cross-references point you to related material whenever it matters.

## Conventions used in this handbook

To make the handbook easy to scan, the same visual cues appear throughout.

### Callouts

> **Tip** — Helpful suggestions that make a task easier or faster.

> **Note** — Useful background information or context.

> **Warning** — Actions that can cause loss of funds or data. Read these carefully.

### Text styles

- **Bold** marks the names of buttons, fields, menus, and screens you see in the wallet.
- `Monospace` marks file paths, commands, and any text you might need to type exactly.
- *Italics* mark a new term the first time it is introduced.

### Steps and procedures

When the order of actions matters, the steps are numbered:

1. Open the wallet.
2. Click **Send** in the sidebar.
3. Enter the recipient's address.

When the order does not matter, items appear as bullet points.

### Screenshots

Throughout the handbook, screenshots illustrate the screens and dialogs being described, with a caption explaining what each one shows. Because the wallet evolves over time, some screens may differ slightly from those pictured here.

## A word on safety

Phoenix PoCX manages real funds. You alone hold the keys to your wallet, which means you alone are responsible for keeping those funds safe. Two rules are non-negotiable.

> **Warning** — **Write down your recovery phrase the moment your wallet is created, and store it somewhere offline.** Anyone who has the phrase can move your funds. If you lose the phrase and also lose access to this device, the funds cannot be recovered.

> **Warning** — **Never type or paste your recovery phrase into any website, chat, e-mail, or support form.** No member of the Phoenix PoCX project, no exchange, and no support agent will ever ask for it. Anyone who does is trying to steal from you.

Chapter 11 — *Backup, Security & Recovery* — covers these topics in depth.
